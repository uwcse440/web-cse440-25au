It is often difficult for people to reduce their alcohol use, even when they genuinely want
to cut back, because drinking can become habitual and socially reinforced. Existing tools
for managing consumption are often too tedious, or backfire by creating guilt-driven cycles
of motivation and relapse. Additionally, people are not interested manual tracking that
involves high-effort, but more interested in support that helps them stay connected to their
long-term goals.

## Design Walkthrough

    One of our main objectives is to build a tracking system that avoids the issues people
    have with current methods, which include high-effort manual tracking and invasive design.
    Koozie is already popular in drinking contexts and offers a convenient way for users to
    automatically track their drinking goals and those of their friends through a companion
    social app. People can work together along with buddies, which provides incentives for
    users to reflect on and share their progress in a motivating way. Specifically, The main
    purpose of the Koozie is not to serve as a perfect drink counter, but rather to encourage
    mindful drinking and sustained connection to personal and social goals.

    The GoalKeeper Koozie allows for easy, passive tracking that can be fit onto any cup
    shape and size. One of our tasks is to collaborate with friends on alcohol consumption.
    We chose this task because from our findings, we realized that a lot of people depend on
    the people around them to keep them accountable with their drinking habits. So, our
    design supports the user by connecting them with their friends and emphasizes that it is
    not an isolating journey. Our other task is to set and manage personalized drinking goals.
    Each person has their own set of goals that they want to achieve and thus, our app becomes
    a space to reflect and manage said goals. This combination of tasks allows us to design A
    Koozie that is simple, portable, and lightweight for easy tracking, and an app that lets
    the user reflect on their individual and/or shared goals.

    First, users could specify a goal in-app on how much they plan to drink during a given
    time period. During a drinking event, users wrap their GoalKeeper koozies around their
    cup(s), which detects the weight of a cup/drink and tracks the number of drinks from a
    dial the user sets initially. Then the koozie sends their drink info to their phone
    via bluetooth. The koozie acts as a visual reminder of a users goal as they are
    actively drinking that they’ve made commitments and want to keep using it to track their
    consumption.

</ProjectImagesBlock>

<ProjectImageBlock

    }}
    caption="Users can track their drinking progress and communicate with friends on companion App."
    width={6}

>

    After an event, the app stores the koozie drink info and creates a report on the Progress
    page. When the user clicks the Progress button, they can see their drinking habits over
    time and indicated by different colors, and there would be a notification push to the user.
    Furthermore, they can see their friends’ drinking and goals over time if they decided to
    share. Friends that are under or over limits are indicated with color. This lets friends
    monitor in real time, and they can then act on this in a way that suits the needs of the
    friend. After the goal time is over, the user can update their goal for next time in the
    Set a Goal page.

</ProjectImageBlock>
